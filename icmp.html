ICMP Header Illustration

The Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP) is used by network devices, such as routers, to send error messages and operational information indicating success or failure when communicating with another IP address. ICMP is mainly used for diagnostic or control purposes, or generated in response to errors in IP operations. The most well-known ICMP messages are "ping" and "traceroute".
