IPv6: The Future of Networking

A Professional Instructional Guide for Cybersecurity & AI Students

1. Why the Shift?

IPv4 uses a 32-bit address space, allowing for approximately 4.3 billion addresses. In our world of IoT and AI-driven edge devices, we ran out. Enter IPv6: a 128-bit powerhouse.

The Scale: IPv6 provides $2^{128}$ addresses.
That is approximately 340 undecillion addresses.

2. Anatomy of an Address

IPv6 addresses use 8 groups of 4 hexadecimal digits, separated by colons. Click the button to see how we shorten addresses using the Zero Compression rule.

Full Address: 2001:0db8:0000:0000:0000:ff00:0042:8329

Compressed: 2001:db8::ff00:42:8329
