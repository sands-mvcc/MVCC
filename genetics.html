About Mendelian Inheritance

Mendelian inheritance describes how traits are passed from parents to offspring through genes. Gregor Mendel discovered that traits are inherited in predictable patterns based on dominant and recessive alleles.

Key Concepts:

  • Alleles: Different versions of a gene (e.g., B for brown eyes, b for blue eyes)
  • Dominant Allele (Capital Letter): Masks the recessive allele and is expressed in the phenotype
  • Recessive Allele (Lowercase Letter): Only expressed when two copies are present
  • Genotype: The genetic makeup (e.g., BB, Bb, bb)
  • Phenotype: The observable trait (e.g., brown eyes, blue eyes)
  • Homozygous: Two identical alleles (BB or bb)
  • Heterozygous: Two different alleles (Bb)
