Dr. John L. Sands stands as a pioneering figure in cybersecurity education, having dedicated over three and a half decades to advancing the field through innovative teaching, substantial research funding, and transformational leadership. His work has not only shaped cybersecurity education at Moraine Valley Community College but has created ripple effects throughout the national educational landscape.

Awards & Recognition

2024 ACCT National Faculty Member Award

Received the prestigious Association of Community College Trustees (ACCT) National Faculty Member Award, recognizing outstanding contributions to community college education and student success.

2020 AACC Dale P. Parnell Faculty Distinction Recognition

Honored by the American Association of Community Colleges (AACC) with the Dale P. Parnell Faculty Distinction Recognition, celebrating excellence in teaching and educational innovation.

2019 Professor of the Year

Named Professor of the Year at Moraine Valley Community College, acknowledging his exceptional dedication to student learning and academic excellence.

These awards reflect Dr. Sands' commitment to educational excellence and his significant impact on both students and the broader academic community. His recognition spans local, state, and national levels, demonstrating the far-reaching influence of his work.

Leadership Roles

NCyTE Center Co-Principal Investigator

Serves as Co-Principal Investigator for the National Cybersecurity Training & Education (NCyTE) Center, a national initiative focused on advancing cybersecurity education and workforce development.

CSSIA Founder

Founded the Center for Systems Security and Information Assurance (CSSIA), establishing a hub for cybersecurity education, research, and professional development.

3CS Co-Founder

Co-founded the Community College Cybersecurity Symposium (3CS), which has grown from a small gathering to an annual event attracting over 500 participants from across the nation.

Competition Development

Dr. Sands has been instrumental in establishing and promoting cybersecurity competitions that challenge students and advance the field:

  • National Cyber League: Helped establish this premier cybersecurity competition
  • Regional CCDC Events: Developed and supported Collegiate Cyber Defense Competition events
